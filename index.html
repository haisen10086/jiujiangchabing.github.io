Jiujiang Tea Cakes are fragrant, crispy and delicious. They are sweet but not cloying, and are truly fine products among tea snacks!

Introduction and Characteristics of Jiujiang Tea Cakes

Jiujiang tea cake is a characteristic traditional spot in Jiujiang area, Jiangxi, which originated in the Tang Dynasty and prospered in the Ming and Qing dynasties, with a history of 1,000 years. It has the characteristics of "small and fine, thin and crispy, crispy and sweet, fragrant and beautiful", and is a good product in refreshments. High-quality flour, sesame, sugar, tea powder and other raw materials are selected, and after careful blending and production, delicious Jiujiang tea cakes can be made.

The Ways to Eat Jiujiang Tea Cakes

  • Pair it with Lushan Yunwu Tea, which is local to Jiujiang. The delicate fragrance of the tea complements the sweet flavor of the Jiujiang tea cakes.
  • It can be paired with milk and served as part of breakfast, which is both nutritious and delicious.
  • During afternoon tea time, pair it with coffee and enjoy a pleasant moment.
